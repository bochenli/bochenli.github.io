Brief Bio

I am a full-time Research Scientist at the ByteDance/TikTok. I obtained my PhD degree from the Audio Information Research Lab of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Rochester in October 2020, under the supervision of Professor Zhiyao Duan, with the Outstanding PhD Dissertation Award.

My research interests lie primarily in the inter-disciplinary area of audio signal processing, machine learning, human-computer interaction and computer vision towards multimodal analysis of music. The long-term goal of my research is to design intelligent systems to help people interact with music. Specific problems that I have been working on include audio-visual singing voice separation, multi-model music performance analysis, cross-modal generation and retrieval, etc.

During my employment at ByteDance/TikTok, I conduct research about multi-modal music systems and interactive design for music creation and consumption, with the context of a number of content creation platforms (e.g., TikTok, Resso, CapCut, etc.) providing testbeds consisting of over a billion users.
